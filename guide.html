How to update your website

Everything on your site — your writing, your photos, your projects — you can change yourself, any time, from one simple page. No code, ever.

You can’t break anything. Every change is saved with a full history, so an older version can always be brought back. Explore freely.

Making a change, start to finish

1

Open your editor

Go to yourwebsite.com/admin (add /admin to the end of your site address).

Click Login with GitHub and sign in with your own account. You only do this the first time on a device.

2

Pick the section you want to change

Click Records & Tracklists, then choose a section — About, Copywriting, Visual Work, Sound, or Film. Each one is a page on your site.

3

Edit it

Change any text by typing over it. To add work to a project, use the boxes for:

Photo Short video PDF Link Description

Drag a photo or file straight into its box, or paste a link (like a YouTube video). Everything is optional — add only what you have.

4

Save it

Click Save (or Publish). Wait about a minute, then refresh your website — your change is there, live for everyone.

Good to know

  • Photos: JPG or PNG work best.
  • Video: upload short clips as .mp4 (they play everywhere). Avoid .mov — many browsers won’t play it. For longer videos, paste a YouTube or Vimeo link instead of uploading.
  • Order: the little code on each section (A1, A2…) sets the order they appear in on your home screen.
  • About page extras: the Education and Certifications boxes are only used by the About section — leave them empty on the others.
  • Nothing is permanent: every save is remembered, so a past version can always be restored.
Whenever you’re ready to change something, just open /admin and go. It’s yours.
